Interpretation Mechanism At heavy mass (κ=0.12346) At chiral limit (κ→κ_c^ren) Status
(a) Gauge-link color-spin entanglement Each hopping step (1±eμ)⊗Uμ(x) entangles the spinor's H-eigenspaces with the gauge field. On a single config, X_int is a full 12×12 matrix, not proportional to I₁₂. The deviation from dim(r)/8 measures how much the accumulated product of gauge links has broken the free-field isotropy in the (spin×color) space. With 20 configs this entanglement averages only partially to zero. Primary driver. Perturbative in g²; each hop contributes O(g²) to off-diagonal X_int. Grows with t as more links are traversed. Suppressed by N_cfg → ∞. Still present but dominated by (c). The propagator at κ_c^ren is long-range, so many more links contribute — larger per-config entanglement. Primary at heavy mass
(b) Lattice artifact (Wilson term) The Wilson mass term r Σμ(1−cos pμ) breaks Lorentz symmetry explicitly at O(a). This introduces a small non-isotropic contribution to X_int that is not present in the continuum Dirac operator. Does not vanish with N_cfg but does vanish in the continuum limit (larger β, finer lattice). Subleading. On a N=6 lattice at β∈{4.5,6.0}, the O(a) corrections are at the few-percent level of the leading physics. Their contribution to the grade-fraction deviations is at most comparable to (a). Still present; cannot be separated from (a) without a continuum-limit study at multiple β with fixed physical mass. Subleading, always present
(c) Non-perturbative vacuum dressing If the chiral condensate or confining flux couples differently to the triplet vs singlet H-eigenspaces, the asymptotic (large-t) grade fractions of the pion ground state will differ between phases. This is a genuine non-perturbative signal — not removable by more statistics, and not a lattice artifact. Suppressed. At m₀=0.05, the quark mass in lattice units is m_phys ≈ 0.76. Non-perturbative chiral effects scale as (m_π/Λ_QCD)² and are tiny at this mass. No evidence at 0.6σ. This is the entire point of Experiment B. At κ→κ_c^ren the pion becomes light and non-perturbative dressing of the grade channels is no longer suppressed. Suppressed at heavy mass Target of Exp. B

At t=1 you are measuring the propagator one step from the source (dominated by short-range gauge fluctuations). At t=N/2=3 you are at the temporal midpoint where backward-propagating states from the other side of the periodic lattice arrive with equal weight. The asymptotic grade fraction of the pion ground state (large t) need not match the t=1 fraction. However, with m_π·L ≈ 10, the signal at t=3 is noise-dominated. The sign flip is most likely a finite-sample fluctuation, not a physical sign change.

What §6.7 should say about the deviations. The ~10⁻⁴ deviations from dim(r)/8 seen in Experiment A are consistent with finite-sample fluctuations (N_cfg=20) of gauge-link color-spin entanglement — interpretation (a) operating perturbatively at heavy mass. The constraint-respecting sign pattern (Δf₃ = −Δf₃̄, Δf₁ₐ = −Δf₁ᵦ) is algebraically forced and carries no additional information. The 3.6σ deviation at t=3 (β=6.0) is on a timeslice where m_π·t ≈ 10 and signal-to-noise is severely degraded with 20 configurations; it cannot be claimed as a physical signal at current statistics. The cleanest near-term test — a free-field run and N_cfg scaling — costs nothing algorithmically and would definitively separate (a) from (b). Interpretation (c) requires Experiment B at κ→κ_c^ren and is the physically interesting hypothesis that the current data neither confirms nor rules out.
