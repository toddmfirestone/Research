Is this a known structure? Yes — three places in the literature

SU(4) ⊃ SU(3)×U(1) branching rules
Standard representation theory; any GUT textbook		 The fundamental 4 of SU(4) decomposes under the maximal-rank subgroup SU(3)×U(1) as: 4 → 3₊₁/₃ ⊕ 1₋₁ (using U(1) charge Q = (2/3)H). The 4̄ decomposes as 4̄ → 3̄₋₁/₃ ⊕ 1₊₁. This is a standard weight diagram of SU(4) under its maximal-rank subgroup. Γ₇ labels which of the two representations (4 or 4̄) the state lives in. H labels the U(1) charge within that representation. Together they give the complete branching rule. Known — textbook
Pati-Salam SU(4)_color model (1974)
Pati & Salam, Phys. Rev. D10, 275 (1974)		 In Pati-Salam unification, quarks and leptons are unified under SU(4)_color where the lepton is the "fourth color." The SU(4) → SU(3)×U(1)_{B-L} decomposition gives: quarks with B−L = 1/3, leptons with B−L = −1. The U(1) generator is precisely H (up to normalization: Q_{B-L} = (2/3)H). The two singlets 1a,1b correspond to lepton and antilepton. H eigenvalue of ±1/2 for triplets, ±3/2 for singlets maps to B−L charge with normalization (2/3)H = B−L. This is the first physical context where this specific double-grading structure appeared. Known — 1974
Furey's algebraic classification program
Refs [20–24] in Paper 1		 Furey explicitly identifies the 8-dimensional minimal left ideal of Cl(6) with the spectrum of a single generation of Standard Model particles: 3 quarks, 3 antiquarks, 1 lepton, 1 antilepton. The grading operators used are precisely the analogs of Γ₇ and H. The SU(3)×U(1) identification is the same. The (Γ₇, H) double grading in Paper 2 is the same structure Furey uses to classify particles algebraically. The difference: Furey works in the algebraic/kinematic setting; Paper 2 computes dynamics on a gauge ensemble. Known — Furey 2012–18
Does it have a name? — what the representation theory literature calls it
The (Γ₇, H) pair is a complete set of commuting observables (CSCO) for the SU(3) representation content of the Spin(6) ≅ SU(4) Dirac spinor.

More formally: the full Cartan subalgebra of Spin(6) (rank 3) is spanned by {B₁₂, B₃₄, B₅₆}. The three weight coordinates of a state in the spinor representation are its eigenvalues under these three generators. The SU(3) representation label requires one combination (H = B₁₂+B₃₄+B₅₆, the "trace" — the U(1) not in SU(3)), while the chirality label requires Γ₇ = iI₆ (the Spin(6) pseudoscalar, not a Cartan element but a central element). Together they uniquely identify which of the four SU(3) irreps a state belongs to.

In the GUT and group theory literature, this is called the SU(4) ⊃ SU(3)×U(1) weight decomposition of the fundamental (spinor) representation. In the language of Dynkin diagrams, SU(3)×U(1) is the Levi subgroup of the maximal parabolic subalgebra of A₃ (= SU(4)) obtained by removing the first Dynkin node. This is a standard construction and fully documented in Slansky's 1981 Physics Reports review of group theory for unified models.

The complete algebraic picture — what the (Γ₇, H) grading is and is not
The (Γ₇, H) double grading... Is Is not
Origin in Lie theory The standard weight decomposition of the 8-dim Dirac spinor of Spin(6) ≅ SU(4) under the Levi subgroup SU(3)×U(1). Fully determined by the embedding chain SU(3) = Stab_{SU(4)}(ξ₀). A new mathematical structure. It is a known consequence of SU(4) ⊃ SU(3)×U(1) branching, appearing in Slansky (1981), Pati-Salam (1974), and Furey (2012–2018).
Relation to Cartan subalgebra H = B₁₂+B₃₄+B₅₆ is in the Cartan subalgebra of Spin(6) (rank 3; all three bivectors commute). The (B₁₂, B₃₄, B₅₆) triple is the full Cartan data; H is the one combination that commutes with SU(3). Γ₇ = iI₆ is NOT in the Cartan subalgebra — it is a central element of Spin(6), lying in the center ℤ₂ of Spin(6)/SO(6). The (Γ₇, H) pair mixes Cartan data with central data.
Completeness as a label Complete for identifying which SU(3) representation a spinor state belongs to: (Γ₇, H) uniquely determines the rep from {3, 3̄, 1a, 1b}. Complete for identifying which state within a representation. To distinguish the three states inside the triplet 3 (the three "colors"), you need the full Cartan data (B₁₂, B₃₄, B₅₆) or equivalently the SU(3) weight coordinates (T₃, T₈ eigenvalues).
Novel contribution of Paper 2 First dynamical computation using this grading. The grade-projected pion correlators C_π^(r)(t) implement the (Γ₇, H) decomposition on a Monte Carlo gauge ensemble. The completeness identity ΣC_π^(r) = C_π holds to 5.4×10⁻¹⁶. No prior work has computed dynamics in this grading. A discovery of the grading itself. The algebraic structure is known. The claim is that it works dynamically in a Wilson lattice QCD setting — that the projectors commute with γ₅, are SU(3)-covariant, and produce a well-defined and complete decomposition of the pion correlator.
What §6.7 should say. The (Γ₇, H) double grading is the standard weight decomposition of the Spin(6) ≅ SU(4) Dirac spinor under the Levi subgroup SU(3)×U(1). This structure is known in the representation-theory literature (Slansky 1981) and appears physically in the Pati-Salam SU(4)_color model (1974), where H generates the B−L symmetry up to normalization. In the algebraic classification program of Furey [20–24], the same decomposition identifies the SU(3) quantum numbers of a single generation of Standard Model fermions. The contribution of the present work is not the discovery of this grading but its first dynamical implementation: the grade projectors P_r are constructed as Lagrange spectral projectors of H, verified to satisfy all required algebraic identities to residual ≤ 2.4×10⁻¹⁵, and used to decompose a physical pion correlator on a Monte Carlo gauge ensemble. The label Γ₇ = "Weyl chirality of Spin(6)" is exact; the label H = "Cartan hypercharge" should be stated precisely as the generator of the U(1) commutant of SU(3) in SU(4). The structural analogy between 1a,1b and lepton quantum numbers in the Furey program is noted but does not apply in the dynamical QCD context of this work, where color is carried by a separate ℂ³ factor.
