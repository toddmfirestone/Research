How many independent observables? — counting carefully
Starting from four fractions {f₃, f₃̄, f₁ₐ, f₁ᵦ}, each constraint removes one degree of freedom:

Constraint 1 — Completeness (algebraic, holds on every gauge configuration):
f₃ + f₃̄ + f₁ₐ + f₁ᵦ = 1  →  removes one. Three independent.

Constraint 2 — C-symmetry of the ensemble (holds in the N_cfg → ∞ limit with C-symmetric Wilson action and C-symmetric point source):
⟨f₃⟩ = ⟨f₃̄⟩ and ⟨f₁ₐ⟩ = ⟨f₁ᵦ⟩  →  removes two more. ONE independent observable survives.

That surviving observable is: f_triplet = f₃ + f₃̄ (equivalently, f_singlet = f₁ₐ + f₁ᵦ = 1 − f_triplet). The C-odd quantities A_color and A_singlet both vanish exactly in the ensemble limit. The brief's statement of "two independent observables" refers to per-configuration fluctuations — in the ensemble limit there is only one.
The complete observable structure — four natural combinations
f_triplet = f₃ + f₃̄
C-even, survives ensemble average
The fraction of pion correlator weight in the color-carrying channels. Free-field value: 3/4. In the ensemble limit this is the ONLY nonzero observable. It measures how much of the pion correlator lives in the triplet vs singlet spinor subspaces. This is what Experiment B will test: does f_triplet shift between β=4.5 and β=6.0 near the chiral limit?
f_singlet = f₁ₐ + f₁ᵦ
C-even; = 1 − f_triplet by completeness
Not independent — determined by f_triplet. Free-field value: 1/4. Included here for completeness: a shift toward singlet channels would indicate the gauge dynamics redistributes weight from color-carrying to color-neutral spinor components. This is the C-even content of the (Γ₇,H) decomposition.
A_color = f₃ − f₃̄
C-odd; → 0 in ensemble limit (θ=0)
Asymmetry between triplet (quark) and anti-triplet (antiquark) channels. Vanishes in C-symmetric limit. Per-configuration nonzero due to finite-sample C-violation. NOT parity-violating — see below. In a CP-violating vacuum (θ≠0), could be nonzero in the ensemble limit as a probe of CP violation through the grade structure.
A_singlet = f₁ₐ − f₁ᵦ
C-odd; → 0 in ensemble limit (θ=0)
Asymmetry between the two singlet channels (opposite Γ₇). Vanishes in C-symmetric limit. Also NOT parity-violating. Could couple to topological charge near the chiral limit if Dirac zero modes have a preferred Spin(6)-chirality — but this is conjectural and inaccessible at heavy mass.

Spatial reflection x⃗ → −x⃗		 P acts on the spacetime structure of the correlator: C_π(t) → C_π(t) for the zero-momentum pion (spatial sum absorbs the parity flip). CP combined In standard QCD with θ=0: CP is a symmetry of the vacuum. A_color = 0 in the ensemble limit follows from C alone (even without P). If θ≠0 (CP violation): the ensemble is not C-symmetric, and ⟨A_color⟩ could be nonzero. Time reversal T T is an antiunitary symmetry. For the zero-momentum pion correlator with periodic boundary conditions, T maps t → N−t (lattice reflection). This is a symmetry of the ensemble average and does not mix grade channels. A_color unchanged under T (same argument as P — T acts on spacetime, not on internal grade structure). T-even
Correction to the brief. The question asks "Is A_color a parity-violating observable?" The answer is no. A_color is C-odd and P-even (and T-even). It is not a probe of parity violation. It is a probe of C-violation — which in standard QCD with θ=0 means it vanishes in the ensemble limit and what we see is purely a finite-sample artifact. §6.7 should not describe A_color as parity-related.
Is A_singlet sensitive to topological charge or the η' anomaly?
Mechanism Argument for sensitivity Why it is suppressed here Status
Dirac zero modes
(Atiyah-Singer index theorem)		 In a gauge background with topological charge Q, the Dirac operator has exactly |Q| zero modes of definite 4D chirality (γ₅ eigenvalue). These zero modes contribute to the singlet pion correlator (the flavor-singlet channel) but not to the flavor-triplet channel — this is the mechanism behind the U(1)_A anomaly mass of the η'. If zero modes also have a preferred Spin(6) chirality (Γ₇ eigenvalue), they would contribute differently to f₁ₐ (Γ₇=+1) vs f₁ᵦ (Γ₇=−1), generating A_singlet ≠ 0 in nontrivial topological sectors. We are computing a flavor-non-singlet pion (the isospin triplet π), not the η'. The zero-mode contribution to the non-singlet pion correlator is suppressed. At κ=0.12346 (heavy mass), the propagator never approaches the zero-mode regime. The quenched approximation also removes fermion loop contributions that carry the anomaly in the dynamical theory. Conjectural — requires chiral limit + topology separation
Spin(6) chirality of zero modes Atiyah-Singer guarantees γ₅ chirality of zero modes. But does a Cl(6) zero mode also carry a definite Γ₇ eigenvalue? Since Γ₇ = γ₅·(ie₅e₆) and γ₅ is fixed on zero modes, a definite Γ₇ eigenvalue requires also a definite ie₅e₆ eigenvalue — i.e., the zero mode must be an eigenstate of the internal chirality (e₅,e₆) as well. In the Cl(6) free field (no gauge links), this holds: D_W^free zero modes (at κ_c, m₀=0) are eigenstates of both γ₅ and ie₅e₆. Whether this persists in the interacting theory is an open question. Cannot be tested with current data (heavy mass, no topological sector separation). Requires: (1) κ→κ_c^ren (chiral limit), (2) separate gauge configs by topological sector Q, (3) sufficient statistics to resolve A_singlet in each sector. Open question — Experiment B prerequisite
Direct η' anomaly coupling The U(1)_A anomaly generates a mass for the η' through the Veneziano formula: m²_η' ∝ χ_top/f²_π where χ_top = ⟨Q²⟩/V is the topological susceptibility. In the grade-decomposed language, if the η' wavefunction has preferential Γ₇ structure, then A_singlet could probe χ_top through the singlet channel weight. We are computing a non-singlet pion correlator in the quenched approximation. The quenched theory has a pathological hairpin contribution to the η' that is absent here. The non-singlet pion is insensitive to χ_top at leading order. Any sensitivity would appear at O(1/N_c) or higher. Suppressed — quenched + non-singlet

What §6.7 should say about channel-pair structure. The anti-correlation Δf₃ = −Δf₃̄ and Δf₁ₐ = −Δf₁ᵦ is not merely an algebraic curiosity — it is the statement that C-symmetry violation in the ensemble enters as a single independent degree of freedom, not two. In the θ=0 quenched Wilson ensemble with C-symmetric point source, both A_color = f₃−f₃̄ and A_singlet = f₁ₐ−f₁ᵦ vanish in the N_cfg→∞ limit. The values observed with N_cfg=20 (magnitude ~1.6×10⁻⁴) are consistent with finite-sample C-breaking and are expected to scale as 1/√N_cfg. A_color is C-odd and parity-even — it is not a probe of parity violation. The sole C-even observable that survives the ensemble limit is f_triplet = f₃+f₃̄ (the total weight in the color-carrying channels), and the phase difference f_triplet(β=4.5) − f_triplet(β=6.0) is the target observable for Experiment B. A_singlet's potential sensitivity to topological charge through Spin(6) chirality of Dirac zero modes is a physically motivated conjecture that requires the chiral limit, topological sector separation, and O(100+) configurations to test — none of which are available in the current dataset.
