The decisive algebraic obstruction
The grade projectors are Lagrange spectral projectors of H:
P_r = ∏_{s≠r} (H − λ_s·I₈) / (λ_r − λ_s)

H = B₁₂ + B₃₄ + B₅₆

B₅₆ = (i/4)[e₅, e₆]

Standard 4-component Dirac algebra: generators {γ₁, γ₂, γ₃, γ₄} only.
e₅ and e₆ do not exist in this algebra.
Therefore B₅₆ does not exist. Therefore H does not exist. Therefore P_r does not exist.
This is not a matter of convenience or notation. B₅₆ is structurally necessary: it is the bivector that labels the third qubit in the tensor product ℂ²⊗ℂ²⊗ℂ², which is the qubit that distinguishes the singlet states (H=±3/2) from the triplet/antitriplet states (H=±1/2). Without e₅ and e₆, there is no third qubit, no B₅₆, no H, and no grade decomposition. The standard 4-component algebra Cl(4) simply does not have room for this structure.

The precise novelty statement for §6.7
Question Answer
Can C_π^(r)(t) be measured in standard 4-component Wilson QCD? Not naturally. The projector P_r requires the Cartan element H = B₁₂+B₃₄+B₅₆, which in turn requires the generators e₅ and e₆ that do not exist in the standard 4-component Dirac algebra Cl(4). A sufficiently informed practitioner could write down the explicit 8×8 matrix for P_r (after computing it in Cl(6)) and use it as an ad hoc 8×8 operator — but this imports the Cl(6) structure by stealth and provides no algebraic understanding of what P_r means or why it is gauge-invariant.
Is the projector construction natural only in Cl(6)? Yes. The projectors arise as Lagrange spectral projectors of H, a specific Cartan element of Spin(6) ⊂ Cl(6). The naturalness derives from the fact that H is part of the algebra's own structure — it does not need to be imposed externally. In the standard formulation, no analog of H exists because e₅ and e₆ are absent. Furthermore, the grade structure cuts across the γ₅ chirality decomposition (the triplet and antitriplet each contain states of both chiralities), making it impossible to express P_r as any combination of standard Dirac projectors.
What does Cl(6) buy beyond a renaming? Three concrete things. First: the internal generators e₅,e₆ — and therefore B₅₆ — which are necessary and sufficient to construct H and all four projectors P_r. Second: the algebraic guarantee that the projectors are gauge-invariant ([T_a, P_r] = 0), complete (ΣP_r = I₈), and compatible with chiral structure ([γ₅, P_r] = 0) — all following from the Cl(6) algebra without separate proof. Third: the physical interpretation through the (Γ₇, H) double-grading, which identifies each grade channel as a specific SU(3) representation subspace of the Spin(6) spinor — a labeling that has no standard QCD expression. What Cl(6) does NOT buy: new physics beyond standard QCD, computational efficiency, or results for standard observables (plaquette, GOR, κ_c) that differ from the standard formulation.
What §6.7 should say. The grade-projected correlator C_π^(r)(t) is a gauge-invariant physical observable — gauge invariance follows from [T_a, P_r] = 0, verified to 2.4×10⁻¹⁵. It cannot be naturally expressed in standard 4-component Wilson lattice QCD for a specific algebraic reason: the Cartan element H = B₁₂+B₃₄+B₅₆ requires the bivector B₅₆ = (i/4)[e₅,e₆], which involves generators e₅ and e₆ absent from the standard Dirac algebra Cl(4). The grade structure also cuts across the standard Lorentz chirality decomposition — the triplet and antitriplet subspaces each contain states of both γ₅ chirality — so no combination of standard Dirac projectors can reproduce P_r. An explicit numerical equivalent could in principle be constructed by reading off the Cl(6) matrices and using them as ad hoc operators, but this construction is unmotivated, uninterpretable, and algebraically unjustified without the Cl(6) framework. The Cl(6) formulation is therefore not a renaming: it provides the internal generators e₅,e₆ that enable the grade decomposition, the algebraic guarantees (completeness, gauge invariance, γ₅-compatibility) that follow automatically from the algebra, and the (Γ₇, H) interpretation that identifies the physical content of each channel. These have no standard QCD equivalents. What is shared with standard QCD: all gauge-sector and standard fermionic observables (the 16/16 benchmark equivalences of Paper 1). What is specific to Cl(6): the grade decomposition C_π^(r)(t) and the observables derived from it.
