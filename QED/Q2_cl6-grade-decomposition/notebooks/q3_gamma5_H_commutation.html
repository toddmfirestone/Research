Stage 1 — the explicit matrices, from first principles
1
Compute γ5 in the tensor product representation. Using the Pauli tensor product basis:
e₁e₂ = (σₓ⊗I₂⊗I₂)(σᵧ⊗I₂⊗I₂) = (σₓσᵧ)⊗I₂⊗I₂ = iσz⊗I₂⊗I₂
e₃e₄ = (σz⊗σₓ⊗I₂)(σz⊗σᵧ⊗I₂) = (σzσz)⊗(σₓσᵧ)⊗I₂ = I₂⊗iσz⊗I₂

γ5 = (e₁e₂)(e₃e₄) = (iσz⊗I₂⊗I₂)(I₂⊗iσz⊗I₂)
= i² · σz⊗σz⊗I₂
= −σz⊗σz⊗I₂ ← acts trivially on the third qubit
2
Compute H in the tensor product representation. Each bivector: Bᵢⱼ = (i/4)[eᵢ,eⱼ] = (i/4)(2eᵢeⱼ) = (i/2)eᵢeⱼ since {eᵢ,eⱼ}=0 for i≠j.
B₁₂ = (i/2)·iσz⊗I₂⊗I₂ = −½ σz⊗I₂⊗I₂
B₃₄ = (i/2)·I₂⊗iσz⊗I₂ = −½ I₂⊗σz⊗I₂
B₅₆ = (i/2)·I₂⊗I₂⊗iσz = −½ I₂⊗I₂⊗σz

H = B₁₂ + B₃₄ + B₅₆
= −½(σz⊗I₂⊗I₂ + I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ + I₂⊗I₂⊗σz)
3
The commutator — explicit check of all three terms.
[γ5, H] = ½[σz⊗σz⊗I₂, σz⊗I₂⊗I₂ + I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ + I₂⊗I₂⊗σz]

Term 1: (σz⊗σz⊗I₂)(σz⊗I₂⊗I₂) − (σz⊗I₂⊗I₂)(σz⊗σz⊗I₂)
= σz²⊗σzI₂⊗I₂ − σz²⊗I₂σz⊗I₂ ← σzI₂ = I₂σz, σz²=I₂
= I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ − I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ = 0

Term 2: (σz⊗σz⊗I₂)(I₂⊗σz⊗I₂) − (I₂⊗σz⊗I₂)(σz⊗σz⊗I₂)
= σz⊗σz²⊗I₂ − σz⊗σz²⊗I₂ = 0

Term 3: (σz⊗σz⊗I₂)(I₂⊗I₂⊗σz) − (I₂⊗I₂⊗σz)(σz⊗σz⊗I₂)
= σz⊗σz⊗I₂σz − σz⊗σz⊗σzI₂ = 0 ← I₂σz = σzI₂
[γ5, H] = 0 exactly.  Verified to 2.4×10⁻¹⁵ in paper
Stage 2 — why this works: the diagonal argument
The proof above is explicit but not illuminating. Here is the reason in one sentence:

In the Pauli tensor product basis, both γ5 and H are diagonal 8×8 matrices.

γ5 = −σz⊗σz⊗I₂ is diagonal: σz = diag(+1,−1), so σz⊗σz⊗I₂ = diag(+1,+1,−1,−1,+1,+1,−1,−1)⊗I₂... more precisely it is block-diagonal with entries ±1 in the standard basis.

H = −½(σz⊗I₂⊗I₂ + I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ + I₂⊗I₂⊗σz) is diagonal: it is a sum of σz in different qubit positions, each of which is diagonal. Its eigenvalues are −½(±1±1±1) ∈ {−3/2, −1/2, +1/2, +3/2}.

Diagonal matrices always commute. That is the entire theorem.

The non-trivial content is not the commutation itself — it is that the specific Pauli tensor product representation simultaneously diagonalises both γ5 and H. A generic grade-4 element of Cl(6) does not commute with a generic grade-2 element. It is only because of the specific bivector structure H = B₁₂+B₃₄+B₅₆ — built from orthogonal, non-overlapping pairs — and the specific construction γ5 = e₁e₂e₃e₄ that both operators are diagonal in the same basis.
Stage 3 — from [γ5, H] = 0 to [γ5, P_r] = 0: one algebraic step
→
The grade projectors are Lagrange spectral projectors of H. With eigenvalues λ ∈ {−3/2, −1/2, +1/2, +3/2}:
P_r = ∏_{s≠r} (H − λ_s·I₈) / (λ_r − λ_s)

Example: P_3 = (H+3/2)(H+1/2)(H−3/2) / [(1/2+3/2)(1/2+1/2)(1/2−3/2)]

P_r is a degree-3 polynomial in H.
[γ5, P_r] = [γ5, f_r(H)] for a polynomial f_r
= f_r commuted through γ5 using [γ5, H]=0 at each step
= 0 for all r ∈ {3, 3̄, 1a, 1b}
If A commutes with H, it commutes with every polynomial in H. The projectors are polynomials in H. QED.

n=4 Cl(8), SU(4)⊂Spin(8) 16 = 4⊕4̄⊕1⊕1⊕6 under SU(4). Six projectors. Yes. γ5 = −σz⊗σz⊗I₂⊗I₂ (grade-4, still same form), H₄ = −½(σz⊗I₂⊗I₂⊗I₂ + I₂⊗σz⊗I₂⊗I₂ + I₂⊗I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ + I₂⊗I₂⊗I₂⊗σz). All diagonal. Commute. SU(4) is the symmetry of a hypothetical 4-color QCD or Pati-Salam. Grade projectors exist and are algebraically clean, but no physical motivation for this case.
General n Cl(2n), SU(n)⊂Spin(2n) 2ⁿ = n⊕n̄⊕ (lower reps) under SU(n) Yes, for all n. In the Pauli tensor product on n qubits: γ5 = −σz⊗σz⊗I₂⊗...⊗I₂ (σz in first two slots, I₂ in remaining n−2), H_n = −½ Σᵢ I₂⊗...⊗σz⊗...⊗I₂ (σz in slot i). Both diagonal. Commute. The γ5 structure is always 4D (spacetime is always 4D). The internal dimension grows with n. The commutation holds universally because γ5 never involves e₅,...,e_{2n}, and H_n is diagonal.
Important caveat: γ5 is fixed as the 4D chiral operator e₁e₂e₃e₄ in all cases — spacetime is always 4D. The generalization is of the internal symmetry (n colors), not of spacetime. The full pseudoscalar I_{2n} = e₁⋯e_{2n} (which is iΓ₇ in Cl(6)) also commutes with H_n by the same diagonal argument, but it is not the physically relevant chiral operator.
What §6.7 should say. The commutativity [γ5, H] = 0 — and therefore [γ5, P_r] = 0 for all four grade projectors — follows from a single structural fact: in the Pauli tensor product representation of Cl(6), both operators are diagonal in the standard 8-dimensional basis. γ5 = −σz⊗σz⊗I₂ and H = −½(σz⊗I₂⊗I₂ + I₂⊗σz⊗I₂ + I₂⊗I₂⊗σz) are simultaneously diagonalised by the computational basis. Since P_r are degree-3 polynomials in H (Lagrange interpolation), [γ5, H]=0 immediately implies [γ5, P_r]=0. The physical meaning is that Lorentz chirality and SU(3) grade are compatible quantum numbers in the Cl(6) spinor space — you can assign definite values to both simultaneously. This is an algebraic necessity for the grade-projected pion correlator to be a well-defined pseudoscalar operator, and it generalises to [γ5, H_n]=0 in Cl(2n) for all n. It is algebraically forced — not a numerical coincidence, and not specific to the choice of lattice parameters.
